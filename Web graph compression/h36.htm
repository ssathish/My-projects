Research Excellence

International Research

We engage across the globe in virtually every area of research. Some areas are embedded within specific institutions but there is also much individual international collaboration.

Some of these collaborations can be seen through exploring our Directory of Experts and International Links or perusing the web pages of specific academic staff.

A number of research projects emerged as a result of the government-funded alliance between Cambridge and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). This is known as the Cambridge-MIT Partnership.

The 2001 Research Assessment Exercise (a UK-wide survey of research) showed that 95% of our submissions were graded as 5 or 5*. This means that at least half of our submitted work was internationally excellent. We are also ranked 2nd in the world after Harvard in both Shanghai Jiao Tong University's "Academic Ranking of World Universities" (2005, 2006) and The Times Higher Education Supplement – Quacquarelli Symonds' "World University Rankings" (2006, 2007).
