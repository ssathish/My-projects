International Cambridge

International Connections

Our international links are extensive, and exist with individual researchers, departments and Colleges. They may involve research collaborations, a regular flow of incoming (usually postgraduate research) students, study abroad opportunities and interactions with industry, government agencies and non-governmental organizations.

In general, agreements are commonly made between foreign insitutions and Cambridge departments or Colleges. Erasmus agreements, which promote mobility and lifelong learning within the European Union, are one such example. The Universities with which we currently have university-level bilateral agreements include:

  • Massachusetts Institute of Technology, United States of America
  • Tsinghua University, People’s Republic of China
  • Peking University (Beida), People’s Republic of China
  • Kyoto University, Japan
  • Tokyo University, Japan

We are also a member of various multilateral alliances, including:

  • Coimbra Group
  • European University Association (EUA)
  • International Alliance of Research Universities (IARU)
  • International Association of Universities (IAU)
  • League of European Research Universities (LERU)

Detailed information about the University's long-standing connections with India can be found at the 'Cambridge India Partnership' website.

Find out more about the University's international activity by exploring our Directory of Experts and International Links - an interactive map and database of just some of the University's many international collaborations and partnerships.
